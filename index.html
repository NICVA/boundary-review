Boundary Review 2018

This site gives information on the provisional proposed constituencies that the Boundary Commission for Northern Ireland have produced. To respond to the consultation (closing 28 November 2016), click here. This is not an official Boundary Commission website.

Under the terms of the 2018 Boundary Review, proposed boundaries have to maintain an 'electoral parity' across the UK. To ensure that there is a similar electorate in each constituency, the number of people registered to vote must be within a range of 5% either side of the UK quota. Based on the number of electors registered on 1 December 2015, this quota is 74,769 electors. New constituencies must therefore have between 71,031 to 78,507 electors. For more information, see the September 2016 report.

If you are interested in "building" new constituencies (or interested in other UK boundary changes) you can use the Constituency Boundaries Tool developed by Oli Hawkins for the House of Commons Library.
